Ghaggar Floods Ravage Haryana and Punjab, Thousands Affected

Heavy rains and breaches in the Ghaggar river have triggered devastating floods across Haryana and Punjab, submerging over 180 villages and destroying nearly 90,000 acres of standing crops. Districts like Fatehabad, Sirsa, and Hisar are the worst hit, with road networks, schools, and irrigation systems severely damaged. Relief efforts are underway, but many families remain stranded as water levels continue to rise. Authorities have urged residents to stay indoors while compensation portals have been opened for farmers, with a deadline of September 10 to file claims. The disaster poses both humanitarian and agricultural challenges for the state governments.

GST Reduction on Renewable Energy Products to Boost Clean Power

In a major reform to accelerate India's energy transition, the government has reduced the Goods and Services Tax (GST) on renewable energy equipment—from 12% to 5%—effective September 22, 2025. The move is expected to lower solar generation costs by 10 paise per unit and wind power by up to 17 paise per unit, making clean energy more affordable for utilities and consumers. By shifting tax burdens onto fossil fuels, the government hopes to promote faster adoption of green energy. Experts say this step will attract more investment into India’s renewable sector and push the country closer to its net-zero goals.

UP Government Announces ₹50 Lakh Aid and Jobs for Martyrs' Families

Reaffirming its commitment to honouring fallen soldiers, the Uttar Pradesh government has announced a comprehensive support package for martyrs' families. Each family will receive ₹50 lakh compensation, a government job, and the dedication of memorials or renamed roads in the martyr's name. The announcement was made by CM Yogi Adityanath during the inauguration of the Gorkha War Memorial project in Gorakhpur. He also declared the renaming of Sainik School, Lucknow after Kargil hero Capt. Manoj Kumar Pandey and the establishment of a new Sainik School in Gorakhpur. The initiative strengthens both military morale and civilian respect for national service.
